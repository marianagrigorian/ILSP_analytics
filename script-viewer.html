Segmented Sales Scripts

EF Adults v5.5 — four buyer segments with full objection handling, conviction lines, fears, never-say rules, and closing techniques.

Show:

3 Principles — Apply to Every Call

1
Start by identifying the segment — "What's prompting you to think about this now?"
2
Anchor your pitch on that segment's Key Selling Points
3
Compete on worth, never on discount. Price is not the conversation — value is.

DO / DON'T Language — All Segments

❌ Never Say✓ Say Instead
"Adult-only" or "adults-only guaranteed" "Like-minded people at a similar life stage" / "a balanced age mix with enough adults to connect with" / "students grouped by level, not age."
"Internationally recognized certification" "A CASC certificate you can put on your CV, showing relevant language skills in a specific field"
"2 weeks" The minimum meaningful stay is 6 weeks. (Default, unless the customer has a constraint)
"Senior" / "elderly" / "for your age" / "still sharp" Address appetite, not age.

01
GRADUATE
Instrumental · career & world unlocking · "The edge your degree didn't give you."

🎙 Tone

▼

Peer-level, energetic, competitive, forward-leaning. A little edge — not safe. Ambitious but NOT corporate. Sound like direct advice from someone two years ahead who is honest about the gap and clear about how to close it.

Never: sound corporate or HR, lead with a discount, or promise a "holiday." Outcome first, always — "got the role," not "my English improved."

👤 Example Persona

▼
S
Sofia, 24
Business degree · 8 months post-graduation · stalling job search
"Three of my friends already have international roles. I've had rejections. I found EF on Instagram — I just don't know if it'll actually help me get a job or if it's an expensive gap year."
She'll raise
Will this get me hired? Parents say get a job now Cost with no income Class with teenagers? What's CASC? 6 weeks too long

💬 Opening Approach

▼
Ask: "What's prompting you to think about this now — graduation, a job search?"
Listen for the gap: degree feels like enough but isn't opening doors. Then pivot:
"The first thing to know is: everyone in your cohort now has the same degree. The question is what makes you the obvious hire. That's exactly what six weeks at EF is designed to answer."

⭐ Key Selling Points

▼
The CASC certificate: a credential you put on your CV showing verified language skills in a specific field. Not a participation award — formal evidence of level.
Career-city immersion (LON, NYC, DUB, BAR) — the city is part of the signal to employers.
Classes formed by level, not age — you're with people at the same crossroads and at your exact level. Teachers are expert communicative-method instructors who specialise in adult progress.
Study & Work programs — (JCG or JCI or INT) let you combine language study with real-world work exposure in-country.
EF's Learning Guarantee: advance one full course level every 6 weeks with full attendance and completed assignments — or you continue studying for free until you do.

🎓 Study & Work Programmes

▼
EF General Job Club (JCG)
Language classes plus structured job-search support: CV workshops, cover letter clinics, interview prep, and career coaching — all in the target language.
EF Intensive Job Club (JCI)
More language hours, same career toolkit — ideal if fluency is the priority and career momentum is tight.
EF Internship Experience (INT)
Language courses combined with a real guaranteed internship placement post course — language skills applied in an actual workplace. The strongest CV story of the three.

💡 Conviction Lines

▼
"Your degree got you to the start line. This is how you get ahead of it."
"Six weeks. A certificate. A story. A network. That's what makes you stand out."
"Everyone has the degree. Almost no one has this."

🚫 Never Say

▼
"Holiday" or "experience" as the hook — outcome first, always
"Adult-only" — say: "you'll be in a cohort at your level, with people at the same crossroads"
"Improve your English" — say: "get the edge", "become the obvious hire", "close the gap"
"2 weeks" — 6 weeks is the default; shorter only if genuinely constrained

😟 Fears & EF Responses

▼
FearEF Response
Will this actually help me get hired? Yes — but lead with proof, not reassurance. CASC certificate and a career city on the CV. A network from 50 countries. That's a hiring story, not a holiday story.
Will I be in a class of teenagers? Classes are formed by level. You'll be with people who tested at the same level — and a significant portion will be at your life stage. Teachers are specialists in adult learning.
My parents say I need a real job now. Frame it as the investment that pays back before the 1st job — cheaper than a postgrad, sharper than a gap year.
Will it be effective? I've tried apps. Immersion produces language progress up to 11 times faster than classroom-only or app-based study. And EF's Learning Guarantee backs it: one level every 6 weeks with full attendance — or you study for free.

⚡ Objection Handling

▼
ObjectionHow to Respond
"Is this going to help me get a job?" Lead with outcomes: "You leave with a CASC certificate, CEFR-verified fluency, and experience in a career city. That's not just better English — it's a hiring story." Then: "What specific role or city are you targeting?"

The teachers are the constant: expert communicative-method instructors who know how to move adults fast."

"Will I get a recognized certificate?" "You'll receive a CASC certificate — a formal credential you put on your CV that shows verified language skills in your specific field, at a certified CEFR level. It's not just a completion certificate; it's evidence of attainment."

🎯 Closing Technique

▼

Close on departure-date urgency. The window shuts when the first job starts.

"The cohorts in [city] for [month] have places now. The students who book before they start working are always the ones who say they'd have waited too long if they hadn't. What's your timeline before the job search gets serious?"

02
PROFESSIONAL
Aspirational · work & life mobility · "You know where you're going. We remove what's in the way."

🎙 Tone

▼

Consultant-direct, deliberate, self-assured, confirmatory. Respects their intelligence and their honest time pressure. Assumes they've already tried alternatives. No soft-selling. No fluff.

Never: use soft language or say "don't worry it'll be fine." Give hard data. Get to outcomes fast. This buyer cuts through pitches — match their pace.

03
RESETTER
Transformational · life & identity reset · "This is not a holiday. It's a decision about who you want to be."

🎙 Tone

▼

Deliberate, self-assured, confirmatory. You are agreeing with a decision they've already half-made — not selling them something new. Warm, never pushy. Respects the weight of the moment.

Never: be salesy or apply pressure. Never raise age proactively. Focus on the experience and community before logistics. Never say "escape", "getaway" or "treat yourself."
