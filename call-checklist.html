Objection Handling

0/8 ▼
✓
Held through at least 3 objections before adjusting course. First = automatic. Second = reflex. Third = real. Most people give up at one.
KEY
✓
Isolated before responding: "Is that the only thing stopping you, or is there something else?" — handle the real blocker, not the stated one
KEY
✓
Labeled the emotion before responding: "It sounds like you've been wanting to do this for a long time but something keeps getting in the way" — named emotions defuse, unnamed emotions become objections
TIP
✓
Used the boomerang where relevant — turned the objection into the reason: "That's exactly why now is the right time, not despite that"
TIP
✓
Used Feel / Felt / Found once if appropriate: "I understand how you feel — others have felt the same. What they found was..."
✓
Mirrored when stuck — repeated the last 3 words of their sentence as a question, then waited in silence
TIP
✓
Named the obvious fear proactively before they did — surfacing it first defuses it and builds enormous trust
✓
Did NOT respond to an objection with another question — answered it cleanly, then asked to check it landed
NEVER

Closing

0/7 ▼
✓
Pre-close check run: "Is there any reason why, if we could sort out X, you wouldn't want to move forward today?" — surfaces hidden blockers before you've committed to your close
KEY
✓
Vivid first-week picture painted — "you arrive Sunday, you're met, Monday morning you're in class, by Thursday you're at dinner with people from 12 countries speaking the language." This closes more than any feature.
KEY
✓
Assumptive close used — not "would you like to go ahead?" but "which works better for you — March or May?" You're not asking if they want to buy; you're asking them to choose between two versions of yes.
KEY
✓
Went silent after the closing question — the first person to speak after a close loses. Asked the question, then stopped completely.
KEY
✓
Closed with specifics — named the destination, the cohort month, the room type, and the start date. Vague closes don't book anything.
✓
Real reason to decide now given — a specific cohort date, availability, a deadline. Never fake it; one true reason beats five manufactured ones.
✓
Introduced the takeaway if stalled: "I want to make sure this is genuinely the right fit for you — it's not for everyone." Paradoxically increases desire and repositions you as an advisor.
TIP

Before You Hang Up

0/5 ▼
✓
Co-decision-maker plan made — armed them with the specific language to persuade their partner, parent, or manager. If someone else will veto this and you have no relationship with them, you've lost.
KEY
✓
Follow-up call booked BEFORE sending anything — "I'll send you the details and we'll go through them together on Thursday at 10" not "I'll send it and you can have a think"
KEY
✓
Specific date, time and agenda confirmed for follow-up — the moment the ball is in their court you've lost control of the sale
✓
Loyal community / return mechanic mentioned at close — "many people come back for their second level, or bring someone next time" — plants the seed of a longer relationship
✓
Ball stays in your court — the next action is yours, not theirs. "I will [send / call / confirm] by [specific day]"
